Community Meeting 

Thank you to everyone who came out for our second Community Meeting last week. These meetings are for you. They provide an opportunity to hear about what is going on at our school,

 express concerns, and ask questions. At the meeting I shared a list of ways our Montessori school is special and have included it below.

 

 What's special about us

* Rather than focusing on test scores, the aim of Montessori education is to foster competent, adaptable citizens who are lifelong learners and problem solvers

 

* Learning occurs in an inquiring, cooperative, nurturing atmosphere as opposed to a competitive classroom where students are ranked against one another

 

* Classrooms that are communities of children and adults working and learning together

 

* Respect and caring attitudes for oneself, others, the environment and all life are fostered


* Learning is active - students have the opportunity to select work and make choices about how to organize their day

* A classroom atmosphere that encourages social interaction with cooperative learning, peer teaching, and emotional development

* A class schedule that allows for large uninterrupted blocks of time to problem solve, to see interdisciplinary connections of knowledge, and to create new ideas

* A diverse set of Montessori materials, activities, and experiences which are designed to foster physical, intellectual, creative, and social independence

* Provides opportunities for service ranging from daily contributions to others within the class or school setting to community outreach programs

* Promotes global awareness and universal values - self respect and the acceptance of the uniqueness and dignity of each person, compassion, empathy, a celebration of diversity, and the development of a global perspective

We are proud to be able to offer this as part of the public school system here in St Augustine.

Eagle Class News:
We have returned to our cosmic tale of the Universe to help the children see the big picture of Earth's history. As the interior of the Earth heated up, the internal rocks and minerals began
to melt, then sort themselves out. The heavier elements went to the center, while the lighter ones migrated towards the surface. From this, we introduce the concept of the layers of the Earth. As our story continues we see a time when the surface of the Earth was a mosaic of rocky islands floating on a sea of lava. This gives the context for the study of volcanoes.

Work continues in our other subject areas with lessons on decimals, number patterns, fact families, time and money, and geometry. Language work includes editing, abbreviations,
sentence analysis, grammar analysis, compound words, spelling, writing a friendly letter, and using commas.  
100th Day of School
Our entire school will celebrate the 100th Day of School next Tuesday, January 29. For homework, each student is asked to use 100 objects to create something. Some examples are: building something with 100 Legos, creating a mosaic with 100 dot stickers, decorate a hat or t-shirt, make a poster, collage, or necklace. Use your imagination! The only rule is that your
creation must contain 100 of something. Each child should think about their progress so far this year and complete the following sentence. "On the first day of school I couldn't __________,
but on the 100th day I can. Bring your creations and completed sentence to school next Tuesday. Additionally, our two classes will work on Monday and Tuesday to perform 100 acts of
kindness. These will be recorded and displayed for all to see.
