Entrepreneurialism

Click here to download a copy of the full article
The current issue of Tomorrow's Child magazine contains an article originally published in Briefings
Magazine (Korn/Ferry International). In it, the Montessori method is described as an  "innovation incubator.... that has spawned a long list of overachievers." This list includes Larry Page & Sergey Brin, Google founders, Jeff Bezos Amazon.com founder, Steve Case, AOL founder, Peter Drucker,
management guru, Wikipedia founder, Jimmy Wales and Will Wright Sim City designer just to name a few. In a 2011 Wall Street Journal blog, Peter Sims wrote, " The Montessori educational approach might be the surest route to joining the creative elite, which is so overrepresented by the schools' alumni that one might suspect a Montessori mafia."

The article cites a six year study of 3,000 innovative executives, which discovered "that a significant number of respondents had gone to a Montessori school where they learned to follow their curiosity". Some in the business world believe that the Montessori approach can be used in development programs
for prospective managers and executives. Some of the key attributes of Montessori education are essential for strong leadership.
  • Self-directed learning
  • Focus on problem solving
  • Collaborative learning
  • Comfort with ambiguous situations
  • Self-mastery of concepts
  • Embracing failure as a learning opportunity
Is the Montessori education your child is enjoying today preparing him or her to be the future leader of tomorrow?

Volunteers

Part of the Montessori method involves promoting independence. One aspect of that is to limit the number of adults in the classroom environment. If you would like to volunteer in the classroom please sign up for a specific day and time. That way we are expecting you and can be prepared with a task. Thank you

Eagle Class News

Igneous rocks are formed by volcanoes.  We are taking a look at igneous rocks this week.  Students are doing scientific observation of each rock and recording color, width, length, and weight.  Work with grammar and abbreviations continues along with addition and multiplication on the bead frame.  Our class wrote a collaborative "I wish" poem, and each student will write their own poem.
