Alfie Kohn on Standardized Testing

Click here to learn more about Alfie Kohn
Alfie Kohn is the author of twelve books and numerous essays. He has been described in Time magazine as "the country's most outspoken critic of education's fixation on grades and test scores". Kohn points out that, American students are the most tested in the world. Standardized tests are unusual for children younger than high school in the rest of the world. Most educational specialists condemn the practice of testing children younger than eight or nine. Along the same line, experts and organizations such as the American Psychological Association also condemn the practice of basing promotion on the results of one test.

Standardized tests are based on standards or what Kohn has referred to as the  "bunch o' facts" approach to education. These specific measurable standards are selected on the basis of their testability. Focus on their mastery precludes opportunities for active learning. Meaningful learning is multi-dimensional and includes intrinsic motivation and intellectual exploration. Linda McNeil of Rice University observed that, "Measurable outcomes may be the least significant results of learning."

While we are keeping an eye on the standards here at SAPMS, they are not the focus of our instruction. We strive to stay true to the essence of Montessori education, which I feel is embodied in the following quote from Dr. Montessori. "Our aim is not only to make the child understand and still less to force him to memorize, but to so touch his imagination as to enthuse him to his innermost core."
