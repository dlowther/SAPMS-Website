Montessori Education Week

Since the 1980s the last week in February has been designated as the official week to recognize Montessori education. This year we are celebrating 106 years of Montessori education. Dr. Montessori was a woman ahead of her time. She spent much of her life transforming adults' beliefs about children and their education. Elements of her approach such as, multi-age classrooms, the teacher as guide, developmentally appropriate and individualized education, and the use of manipulative learning materials have been adapted in modern education.

"My experiments conducted in many different countries, have been going on for forty years (106 now), and as the children grew up, parents kept asking me to extend my methods to the later ages. We then found that individual activity is the one factor that stimulates and produces development, and that this is not more true for the little ones of preschool age than it is for the junior, middle, and upper school children." -Maria Montessori

In honor of Montessori Education Week, I am asking our families to tell us about their Montessori school experience. Has it had a positive impact on your child and/or family? Why did you choose Montessori education for your child? What do you like about Montessori education for your child? We will publish your quotes/testimonials in our newsletter I the coming weeks. Please email your thoughts to me.
Great Backyard Bird Count
We all thoroughly enjoyed our walk to count birds. It was a beautiful day with perfect weather. We saw some birds, like the pileated woodpecker, that are not so readily seen. The Eagle class took some time to write  bird inspired haikus. Thanks again to Jenny Holbrook for organizing this event.
