Wise Owl Class 

 

A great way to find out what your child is learning about in class is to ask questions and discuss topics learned in class, here are a few to get you started:

 

Tell me more about rocks and minerals.

 

Explain: What is a synonym?   Give me an example.

 

What are some ways to start a paragraph?

 

How do you round a number to the nearest tenth?

 

What are some similarities and differences between people living in Brazil and people living in Florida?

Let's Make the Gala a Big Success!

I hope to see all of our families at the Gala in April. This will be a fun night with good food, good company, and for a good cause. If you haven't gotten your tickets yet, don't wait. Prices go up on Friday.   Our committee is still looking for silent auction items. One way to contribute is to solicit donations around town, or you could create your own basket to donate. Following are some silent auction basket ideas.

  • Pizza or Spaghetti Basket
  • Martini or Margarita Basket
  • Green Clean - eco friendly cleaning supplies
  • Sundae Basket
  • Family Fun Night
  • Movie Night
  • Spa Basket
  • Book Basket

Use your imagination or check online for more ideas. Pack everything in nice basket or container that reflects the theme. Include a list of contents and the basket value. Or consider donating a certificate for a service you could provide like a night or weekend of babysitting, private yoga class, a week of packing someone's school lunch, gift wrapping, calligraphy, or whatever you like to do.

 

We are also going to create class baskets. Each classroom will assemble a themed basket where we ask families to each donate one item or cash to purchase an item. The Owl class will assemble a Beach Basket and the Eagles will do a Garden/Nature Basket. We will send home more information and ideas for these baskets.

 

Our small Gala committee has been working very hard to make this event a success. Please contribute any way you can. Remember your kids are the beneficiaries!

Studio Program

 

I am pleased to announce our new Friday Studio Program. Starting this week, we will alternate park trips with studios. Studios are special enrichment classes. Children choose their studio and commit to two sessions each month. The following month, they will choose again. Some studios will change monthly while others may be ongoing. Following are our studio offerings for this month. Our March studios are: Art with Ms. Heather, Crafts with Ms. Carol, and Gardening with Ms. Judi.

If you would like to offer a studio, it is an easy two session commitment. Please email me with your idea and availability. 

 

Montessori Testimonials

 

"Montessori education for my child means that she strives to do her best work because it makes her happy and fulfilled, not because she is rewarded by outside motivatiors or praise from the teacher.  It means she enjoys choosing the most challenging work available and enjoys both the work itself and its completion.  It means she finds connections between her work subject areas and is able to more deeply understand their importance because they are connected and built upon.  It means she values the success and talents of the whole classroom, because eache child plays an important role as an individual and for the group.  I choose Montessori education for my children because I can, and I'm terriblly grateful to their teachers, the parent volunteer community and the school district for making it possible."   Jean McDowell

 

"I love Montessori because Noah has his interests met on a daily basis, is engaged, is learning so much and loves school.  As a teacher to see my child LOVE school is such a blessing.  He is learning, he is growing.  I can see a huge change in him, so much growth.

Noah said he loves school because it is fun!  I have to say that after six months of kindergarten at the regular public school, my child didn't like school and didn't look forward to it. Now he does."

Leandra Ziecheck
