What's happening at SAPMS!
Cosmic Education

With Montessori's Cosmic Education we give a large narrative framework for many of our science and cultural studies. This is presented through a series of stories known as the Great Lessons. Cosmic Education emphasizes the oneness and interdependency of everything in the Universe. For example,
in the Creation of the Universe lesson we learned that we are all made of the same chemical elements created in the Big Bang and the core of giant stars. The story continues through time to the early earth and studies in geology and geography.

The next classic Montessori "great lesson" is the Story of Life. The three main materials used to tell the story are the Long Black Line, Clock of Eras, and the Timeline of Life. The Long Black Line is 80 feet of black ribbon wound on a spool with a one centimeter strip of white on the inside end. Legend has it that Dr. Montessori used this material to teach humility to a group of Indian children who bragged about their culture's great age and wisdom. As the children unwind the ribbon, the story of the planet is told. The children are instructed to watch carefully for some change in the ribbon that indicates the appearance of human life on Earth. The story continues for a long time, until finally the children spot the white strip. This creates quite an impression! The story is told again using the Clock of Eras which represents the life of the Earth as a 12 hour period on a clock face. A more detailed and elaborate story is told in the Timeline of Life. These lessons are tied into studies in biology.

Following Montessori's teachings, we do not ask children to memorize all the details of these storiesor regurgitate facts. Instead we try to guide them to see patterns and connections among life's many species.

Eagle Class News
 
This week we unroll the Long Black Line and children are exploring the Clock of Eras. Children continue to choose from a number of activities to study South America. These include physical and political, flag studies, and S.A. animal research. We've also enjoyed a few South American dishes prepared by students at home. Geometry work includes the study of lines, angles, triangles, and measuring angles with both the Montessori and common protractor. Division is in the air, with kids using division finger charts, stamp game division, test tube division, and division with no materials at all. We have started reading Mammolina, a biography of Maria Montessori.  Each student needs to choose a biography to read for a book report.  Books should be brought to school on Monday.  Guidelines for the report will come home next week.
