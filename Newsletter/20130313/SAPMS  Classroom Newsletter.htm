What's happening at SAPMS!

Conference Child Care
We will offer child care from 11:45 - 4:00 on the conference days, Wednesday, 3/20 and Thursday, 3/21. The cost for child care is $6/hour. Please let me know if you plan to use this service.  Remember that students participate in conferences.

 

AMS Conference

We have scheduled a day off on Friday to allow our staff to attend the American Montessori Society conference in Orlando. We will attend workshops on a variety of topics such as, Cosmic Education, gardening, fractals, and movement and dance just to name a few. We will also hear keynote speakers including Steven Hughes. Hughes, a pediatric neuropsychologist, will address how Montessori learning environments support the formation of particular aspects of executive functioning. Additionally, we will have to opportunity to meet and network with other Montessori educators. I especially look forward to spending time with Montessori colleagues from around the country. We appreciate the funding we received from SAPMS, which has made our participation possible. We expect to return invigorated and full of new ideas to use in the classroom.

Morning Arrival
Our morning drop off time runs from 8:10 - 8:25. This is when our classroom work cycle begins.  Children arriving after that time are considered tardy. If you arrive after the gate is closed, you must park and walk in with your child.  Late children are to be signed into the late arrival log.  This is located in the binder on the shelf inside the front door.  Children entering the classroom late are disruptive to the learning environment.  Working students are distracted and a teacher must leave her work with the students to open the door.  Thank you for your attention to this.

ART in the PARK
Sunday, April 7th!    
 
As you are all aware the Gala is fast approaching.  Auction items are high on the list of necessities. Beate Schroeder and others led  the children on a Matisse inspired project last year that did quite well at the auction. This year she has another wonderful idea involving the children and art. The inspiration comes from Paul Klee. We will be up-cycling a garden table set using the children's version of Klee's paintings.  We have a bench (donated  by the Schoeder's).  We need a small wooden table in good condition.  If you have one that you would like to donate please let Jeannie Maser know. Other items we need include 2 washable table clothes, various sized paint brushes and sponges (with handles for stencils), wipes for cleaning hands, and newspaper/paper towels.                                                      
                                                         
We would like to do this activity at the Light House park on Sunday April 7th  as a potluck park day as well.   Parents can visit and eat while children play and paint. So plan to bring picnic gear and a dish to share to the park from 12 pm-3 pm.  Have the children wear a paint tee or clothes that can get messy/painty.  Please contact Jeannie Maser at 904-325-0124 if you have any questions or can donate any of the supplies we need. Thank you!
