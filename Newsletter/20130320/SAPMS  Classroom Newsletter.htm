AMS Conference

We had a great time at the conference last weekend and participated in many workshops on a variety of topics including: Supportrting Latecomers to Montessori Classrooms, Using Literature and Experimentation to Learn About Plants, Music & Movements, Making Montessori Work with Common Core Standards. Additionally, we enjoyed a keynote speaker each day. I was especially interested in the talk given by Dr. Steven Hughes, who I mentioned last week.

Hughes, a pediatric neuropsychologist, is head of the Center for Research on Developmental Education and also a Montessori parent. He called Montessori the original brain based method of education presenting a radically different and radically effective educational approach that may be the best method we've got to ensure optimal cognitive, social, and emotional development in every child.

In this age of No child Left Behind and Race to the Top the emphasis in education has become all about conditioning kids to do well on tests. Progress is measured by counting the number of students who are able to provide the expected answers on a standardized test. Dr. Hughes notes that the primary effect of high stakes tests is a narrowing curriculum, less flexibility, less fun, and a decline in creativity and freedom.

He went on to say that the Montessori concept of normalization is analogous to the development of executive functions in the brain which include cognitive flexibility, self control/regulation, working memory, planning , decision making, and inhibitory control. Modern research has validated Montessori's insights by confirming that brain development depends on experiential interaction with the environment. It has shown that the hand is strongest link to child's brain. Or as Dr. Montessori said, "the human brain is hand built."
