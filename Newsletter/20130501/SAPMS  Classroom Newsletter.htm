Three Year Age Grouping and Peer Tutoring
In the Montessori classroom the use of hands on materials encourages learning by imitation. We believe that children learn not by being told but by watching and doing. The three year age grouping allows younger students to learn by observing and imitating the older children. Many of the materials can be used in multiple ways, so younger children observe how a material they use in a more simple way  will be used in an increasingly more complex way as they get older. Seeing the work older students are doing can inspire and motivate the younger. The Montessori teacher is free to work with individuals and small groups because the other children are busy learning from the materials and each other.

Observing others work is an informal form of peer tutoring. More formal peer tutoring also occurs in the classroom when one child shows another how to use a material. Children may also quiz each other on math facts or spelling words. Without grades or other extrinsic motivators, Montessori classrooms are structured in a way that fosters cooperative learning. Children learn naturally from one another by asking, and the Montessori teacher may ask children to help each other with their work.

Service Hours
Your Contract of Commitment requires each family to contribute 20 hours of service to the school each school year. Alternatively, this commitment can be paid off at the rate of $20/hour. We know that many of you have far exceeded this 20 hour requirement, and we thank you for your service. We will be going through the volunteer tracking forms soon. Please make sure your service hours have been recorded in the binder at the entrance to the school. These forms are in the back of the binder organized alphabetically by last name. It is the responsibility of each family to record service hours. Hours are given for time in the classroom, chaperoning a field trip, working on a committee, time spent helping with the Gala, or for donations to the school. Donations are figured at the above rate of $20/hour. Receipts must be included to document the donation. Thank you for your prompt attention to this.

Eagle Class News
Children have been busy researching and writing about the different periods of the Paleozoic Era and some of the animals that lived then. This work is integrated with the study of the invertebrate phyla in biology. South American studies continue with work with the study of various biomes. So far we have looked at the mountain biome, wetland biome, and tropical rainforest biome. Food chains and food webs are a new topic of interest. Wow! We introduced the interjection to our grammar studies. Math work includes long multiplication with the bead frame, golden mat, checkerboard, and the big bank game. Children are also working with linear measurement, types of triangles, complimentary & supplementary angles, and abstract division with multiples of ten.
