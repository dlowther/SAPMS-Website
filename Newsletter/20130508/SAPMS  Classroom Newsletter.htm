Art News

P.E. News

The children have been practicing our teamwork skills this month with games, flow yoga, and kickball.


Owl News
We have been researching frogs and their life cycle these past few weeks by recording our observations of our classroom tadpoles.We have integrated this into language arts by reading the Frog in Toad series, and creating individual animal stories based on the series. The children our still very engaged in their studies of South America. They have been working on biome maps and researching animals. In math, we have been working on fraction equivalency, multiplication facts, and story problems.

Community Meeting
Our last community meeting of the year is next Wednesday, May 15. We will meet at the main library adjacent to Carousel Park. We are willing to provide child care at the school, if there is a need. Please email me if you need this service. We are only able to provide child care for children 5years and older. Our environment is not safe for younger children.

Please plan to attend this important meeting. This is your opportunity to hear about what is currently happening at the school and plans for next year. There will be time allotted for you to ask questions or air concerns.


Picasso Exhibit
Our school will attend the Picasso exhibit this Friday, May 10. Permission slips went home on Monday; please let us know if you did not receive yours. The plan is to walk to the Visitor Center, so everyone needs to wear shoes appropriate for walking. The cost for chaperones is $8.50.  Please pay by cash or check made to SAPMS.  Children have free admission. Everyone will bring a lunch to eat in the nearby park.  There will be NO PIZZA on Friday.
  
Students will be divided into two groups to tour the exhibit with a docent. Ms. Heather has worked with the students to prepare them for this experience.

Discovery Education Testing

This will further disrupt our regular school routine. Thank you for your attention to this.

Fundraising Corner:    
Now that the Gala is over, please do not forget about our easy peasy passive fundraisers like Target Redcard "Take Charge of Education" and I-Give for on-line shopping.  Thank you to those that have already been participating in these fundraisers.  We have raised close to $300 this year with the Target Redcard, and close to $100 with I-Give.  It all adds up!

 

Another way to make it add up faster is to get Grandparents and other relatives and friends that do not have kids at other schools enrolled, too.  It is the kind of thing that once they are set up, no other effort is required. 

 

To sign up for Target Redcard, go to https://www-secure.target.com/redcard/tcoe/enroll, enter search by zip code, type in 32084, scroll down and select "St. Augustine Montessori School", click on "select school" and fill in the information requested.  Not only do you save 5% off of all purchases, but an additional 1% of all purchases goes to SAPMS!

 

To sign up for I-Give, go to http://www.igive.com/ and type in exactly "St. Augustine Public Montessori School".  Once you are set up, an icon will appear on your screen when you are shopping on-line with a business that donates to I-Give.  Simply click on the icon to activate the % donation from the business to SAPMS.  When shopping on-line, you can also got to http://www.igive.com/ to see what stores donate through I-Give and what % goes to SAPMS. 

 

Thank you for your continued help and participation with these easy, but important fundraisers!
