Owl News
You can tell that spring is in the air!  Everyone has been observing the changes in our plants and animals around the school.  Tadpoles are growing legs, tomatoes are ripening, sunflowers are blooming!  Students have been observing the changes in weather with our "weather station."  Young writers have been creating their own books, poems and directions for doing an activity.  In Math, story problems have challenged students to use math facts in a practical way.  We look forward to learning and growing more as the school year comes to a close.

Eagle Class News
We are journeying through Amazonia, the world's largest rainforest.  Children are learning about the different plants and animals found in the four layers of the rainforest. We're seeing who eats what in the various rainforest food webs.  Older students are learning how to identify monocots and dicots
according to the numbers of petals in the flowers and the arrangement of the vascular bundles in the stems.  Thank you to Jean McDowell for her popular lessons on the invertebrate phyla.  Each child has chosen an invertebrate animal to research.
