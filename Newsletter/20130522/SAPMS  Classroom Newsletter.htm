What's happening at SAPMS!
Coming Down the Home Stretch
It's hard to believe, but we are down to the last eight days of school. We will be busy with many special activities and projects. Children are busy preparing for a South America Festival which will take place on Monday, June 3rd. Families and friends are invited to this event which will be held during the school day. More details will be provided next week. We are looking forward to our English Tea on May 30. Thank you to Drake's grandmother for bringing this special activity to us.

Our Moving Up Ceremony will be held at 6:00 on Friday, May 31 at the school. At this event we celebrate each student's hard work and dedication to learning. Some of our students will actually be moving into a new class level next year and others will be back in the same class with greater maturity and readiness for new lessons. We will combine this with a pot luck dinner. If you don't have time to prepare a dish, we will need drinks, plates, cups, and plastic ware. No Styrofoam please.

 

Ms. Annette's Spring Concert will be held on Tuesday, June 4th at Moultrie Baptist Church. Our Montessori students will perform with children from the Boys & Girls Club and Ms. Annette's students. There will be a potluck at 5:00 if you would like to join. Otherwise, students should arrive at 6:00. The performance will be at 6:30.

 

The last day of school is June 4th, and this is an early release day. All students will be dismissed at 1:50. There will be no aftercare. We will have one last Pizza Day to give you a break from packing lunch that day.

Art News
The children compared M.C. Escher's geometric tessellation  prints with the organic, curvilinear art of various art nouveau artists. Special attention was given to the numerous lines of symmetry, reflections, and patterns seen in the  art. They soon discovered how challenging and mathematical this type of art is as they created their own individual pattern pieces.
Parent Survey
Please participate in the first Annual Parent Survey for St. Augustine Public Montessori School. Completing this survey will help the Board identify the priorities for the Board, the Director, and staff for the coming school year.

A critical part of any organization's health is having a plan for continuous improvement. Continuous improvement needs to be connected to regular feedback from our parents and staff. In order to do that, we need our parents' participation. We are eager to have a better understanding of our strengths and weaknesses as we plan for our second year, review our organizational practices and celebrate our successes. Over time, annual surveys will help us track our progress.

Below is the link that was emailed to everyone last week to fill out the survey: https://www.surveymonkey.com/s.aspx?sm=cP3WgrUxL4Y7j04Z68nCIQ_3d_3d

The results will be more meaningful if we have 100% participation.
