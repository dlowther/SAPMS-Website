August 22, 2013

Welcome to the New School Year!


The long awaited and much anticipated first day of school has come and gone. We started our new year with an assembly outside with our entire student body gathered together. All the adults were introduced and the returning students gave a rousing welcome to our new students. We talked about the importance of being respectful at our school. Returning and new students shared what being respectful looks like to them. Upon returning to our respective classrooms, we all opened with fun activities to help the children get to know one another. We have a consensus among staff and children that this is going to be a great year!

 

Thank you to everyone who helped us get ready for opening day. Classroom paint was touched up, picnic tables sanded and finished, furniture assembled and moved, building and walkways pressure washed and the grounds cleaned and beautified. We never could have accomplished this without your help. You are greatly appreciated.

 

You will have another opportunity to earn service hours this Saturday. Rob Matthews needs help installing the fence around the Upper Elementary play area. Anyone assisting with this can claim double service hours. Call or text Rob at 904-315-2471.

 

You may be wondering about the police presence in front our school at drop off and pick up the first couple of days. They checked in with us and just want to let parents know that they will be keeping an eye on our school.
FORMS

COMMUNITY MEETING

Our first all school Community Meeting will be held next Wednesday, August 28 at 6:00 in the main library. Childcare will be available at the school for a $2 cash donation. Supervision for children begins at 8:10 in the morning. Children may be dropped off as early as 7:35 for a $5/daily fee. In order to offer afterschool care, we need to have at least 3 students attending on a daily basis. At this time, we have not met this minimum.
BULLYING PREVENTION WORKSHOP

I have invited Leslie Shugart from St. John's County school district to come to our school on Monday, August 26 at 8:30 to speak to our students. She will talk about what bullying is and how to recognize it, what to do if you see someone being bullied or are being bullied yourself. Parents are welcome to attend.
ILLNESS

One of our students was diagnosed with viral meningitis on the first day of school. This form is not highly contagious and most people exposed exhibit mild or no symptoms. Symptoms may include fever, headache, stiff neck, and fatigue. The Center for Disease Control has a comprehensive fact sheet available online.
