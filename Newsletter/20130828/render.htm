Respect

I was pleased to hear Monday's visiting speaker open by talking about respect. Respect is inherent in the child centered Montessori classroom. It starts with the way the adults view the children as individuals with different learning styles and developmental rates. Respect is shown in the care that goes into creating a beautiful learning environment. The aesthetics are carefully maintained by both the children and adults. The children learn to value the quality and beauty that surrounds them. The environment is orderly, and children learn that everything has its own place. Respect is shown by returning materials and supplies to their proper place.

 

Children learn to respect each other. Work mats are used to define their work space. This space is respected as children learn to carefully walk around the mat. Students must ask permission to join friends in their work. They respect one another by taking turns with materials, using quiet voices, and walking in the classroom.

 

Our elementary curriculum expands the child's consciousness of the web of relationships in the universe. Cosmic education emphasizes the fundamental unity of everything in the universe. Respect for their environment comes as they realize that everything is made from the same chemical building blocks that came from the stars. Veteran Montessori educator, Aline Wolf, concludes, "If everything in the universe came about from the same source....then we as human beings are related to all other human beings ,as well as the animals, plants, oceans, and heavenly bodies. Therefore if we deliberately destroy nature or harm other living beings we might ultimately be destroying ourselves. This realization is an underlying principle for promoting peace, equality, and care of the earth." That is the ultimate respect.
