Independence

Facilitating the child's independence is another guiding principle of Montessori education. Classroom design and the prepared environment are key components in this. Materials and supplies are organized on open shelves to provide easy access to the children. Children have the autonomy to choose the activity they would like to use and when they want to do it. When children enter the classroom they know where to put lunchboxes and hang their coats. Independence is achieved by giving children the opportunity to do things for themselves. They learn to be responsible for themselves. This independence and responsibility lead to the development of self-confidence and self-esteem.

 

At our Community Meeting last week there was a question about how to reinforce Montessori learning in the home. Providing opportunities for your child to be independent and assume appropriate responsibilities is the best way to do this. Allow children to carry their own belongings to and from school. Do your children make their own beds, put their dirty clothes in a hamper, and pick up their own toys? Even six year olds can pour their own juice and set the table for dinner. Ask yourself, could my child do this? Don't expect perfection and be prepared to be friendly with error.
UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS
Our older students are enjoying their status as school elders. We eat lunch in a bistro setting with a bird's eye view of the entire back campus. As in the beginning of any school year, students are reviewing concepts with some old and new materials. Literal comprehension skills and fluency are practiced with sentence sequencing cards and scrambled sentences. Work with long multiplication, long division, fractions, and decimals is ongoing. Geometry studies include a review of types of angles and triangles along with an exploration of congruency, similarity, and equivalency. Each student completed a chemical element study and a political map of Europe. We are still in need of a CD player (would love to have some music) and a small refrigerator.
