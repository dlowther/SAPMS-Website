International Day of Peace  


In 1982 a United Nations resolution established the International Day of Peace as a shared day for practical acts of peace and "to devote a specific time to concentrate the efforts of the United Nations and its Member States, as well as the whole of mankind, to promoting the ideals of peace and to giving positive evidence of their commitment to peace in all viable ways." In 2002 the UN General Assembly officially declared September 21 as the permanent annual date for the International Day of Peace.

 

The theme for this year's Peace Day is Education for Peace. This is especially meaningful to us as Montessori education is often referred to as education for peace. Dr. Montessori was not interested in creating an educational model. She was aiming for the reconstruction of human society through education. Throughout her life she worked to further the rights of children, women, and all mankind. She trusted in human's ability to create a better more peaceful society.

 

Her framework for elementary education, Cosmic Education, emphasizes the inter-relationships and interdependency of all life. The plan was conceived during World War II with the idea of educating children to be peaceful citizens of the world. It is a curriculum that promotes values important to humanity like justice and harmony.
