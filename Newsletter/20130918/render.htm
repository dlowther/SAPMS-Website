INTERNATIONAL COASTAL CLEAN UP

Every year the Ocean Conservancy and their Trash Free Seas team help to plan the International Coastal Cleanup and the GTM Reserve needs your help! Meet at the GTM Reserve Environmental Education Center or the GTM Marineland office at 8am to get checked in. This is your opportunity to take part in an International Citizen Science Project. The data from this project goes into an international website on marine debris. We will supply all garbage bags and gloves at either the Environmental Education Center or the Marineland office (depending on where you'd like to participate). Come prepared with a water bottle and sunscreen- be sure to dress for the weather. 

 

I invite you to join in this event at either location. This is a great community service project and a way to empower our children to actively become responsible caretakers of their planet. I will be at the Marineland location and hope some of you can join me.

UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS

The UE students enjoyed a great trip to the GTM Reserve yesterday. WE started with a field experience to the estuary, where we talked about the flora and fauna and the various food webs found there. Then we moved inside for small group lessons on marine debris, horseshoe crabs, and different kinds of echinoderms. Our experience ended with a scavenger hunt around the environmental education center. All agreed that it was a great day. Thanks to our drivers and chaperones, Jenny Holbrook, Nancy Costiera, and Bettina Kelbert. We had fun too!

 

We have added a new enrichment class to the UE schedule. Bettina Kelbert is teaching yoga on Wednesday afternoons. Students are giving this program an enthusiastic two thumbs up! Thanks, Bettina.
