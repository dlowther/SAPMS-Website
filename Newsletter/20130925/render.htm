Peace Day Follow Up  

In the everyday life to the classroom peace is promoted in small but significant ways. The teachers model respect, empathy, and consideration for others, as well as facilitating and encouraging nonviolent approaches to conflict resolution.   Our classrooms place a strong emphasis on collaboration and cooperation. We allow learners to have real responsibilities for helping one another. Our Peace March to the Mission last week allowed our students and accompanying parents the opportunity to join their intentions with   people from 200 countries in support of a culture of world peace. Thanks to all who assisted with our peace flags and joined us on our Peaceful Procession.

CLASSROOM OBSERVATION

Our children have all settled nicely into their classroom routine, and I think that we are able to open up our classroom to observers. When you come into the classroom to observe please follow these guidelines.

 

First of all, silence your cell phone. The teacher will direct you to a seat where you will remain throughout your observation. Do not engage in conversation with any of the children. If approached, say hello then direct the child to resume work. Remember, the teacher is not available to answer questions or have a conversation. Write down any questions that come up, and we will set up a time to discuss them.

 

Notice the environmental design and order in the classroom. Notice how the children work, in small groups, pairs, or individually. Do you see any peer teaching? How do the children interact with each other and with the teacher? See how children take personal responsibility by returning their work to the proper shelf and pushing in their chairs. Plan to come in the morning and spend about an hour. Please contact your child's teacher to make an appointment to observe.

 

If you would like to read more about observation in the Montessori classroom, check out the article on The Full Montessori website.thefullmontessori.wordpress.com.
