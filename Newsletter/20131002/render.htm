FOLDERS

At the beginning of the school year each student was given a clear plastic folder. The folder's purpose is to facilitate home/school communication. In order for this to work, folders must be returned to school each day. Please take the time to review all papers that your child brings home in his or her folder. These papers may include finished work, notes from the teacher or office or homework assignments. Remove these and return the empty folder with your child the next day. Lost or damaged folders are the responsibility of the parent to replace. Thank you for your attention to this.

ARRIVAL & DISMISSAL

There still seems to be a few questions concerning arrival and dismissal times. You have a 15 minute window at each end. Arrival goes from 8:10 - 8:25 allowing classes to get started at 8:30. Students arriving before 8:10 must be accompanied inside by an adult. Please do not just drop children off to enter the building alone. We are not expecting them at that time. There is a $5/day charge for morning extended care. Dismissal is from 2:50 - 3:05 when the gate is locked. Parents picking up after 3:05 will need to park and come inside to pick up. Please do not enter the car line before 2:40 or leave your car unattended. You can find all this information and more in your Community Handbook, which you received last month.
UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS

Work with multiples and factors continues with a variety of activities. Fraction work includes finding equivalences, adding and subtracting fractions with different denominators, and converting fractions to decimals. Transitive and intransitive verbs have been introduced and correlated to student's knowledge of sentence analysis. Our European studies have taken us back in time to ancient Greece. We looked at how the basic fundamental human needs were met in this civilization, along with Greek architecture, and Greek writing. Everyone is enthralled with the story, Black Ships Before Troy. This is an illustrated retelling of Homer's epic poem, The Illiad.
