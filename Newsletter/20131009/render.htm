THE AMERICAN MONTESSORI SCHOOL

I was recently asked the difference between the Association Montessori International (AMI) and the American Montessori Society (AMS). The American Montessori Society was founded in the mid 1960s by Nancy Rambusch.   Although Montessori is seen as a global educator, Rambusch believed that once in America, Montessori's ideas were subject to a transformation within the American culture. AMI schools strive to exactly replicate Dr. Montessori's model, while American followers see her principles and insights as central to the model. Rambusch provided a guide for the authentic American Montessori school by identifying a set of six essential qualities.

  • A Montessori classroom is a child centered learning environment that is responsive to the children's needs, abilities, interests, and potential.
  • Montessori learning is active and filled with developmentally appropriate materials. Learning is self directed and there is liberty within limits.
  • A mixed age learning community that responds to the variety of children's developmental needs. Collaboration and cooperation are stressed rather than competition. Children are encouraged to support each other in their efforts towards mastery.
  • Montessori viewed the child as a spiritual (not religious) being.
  • The Montessori teacher is "firm at the edges and empathetic at the center". The teacher acts as model, observer, and resource consultant.
  • The teacher respectfully engages with the learner, is able to facilitate a match between the learner and knowledge, and is the designer of the prepared environment.
SAPMS is a member of the American Montessori Society and abides by their standards and code of ethics.

For more information about the American Montessori Society visit http://www.amshq.org/
