PARENT EDUCATION EVENING

We will hold our first parent education program on Tuesday, October 29. The topic will be Building the Mathematical Mind. One of the most intriguing areas of the Montessori classroom is the section housing the math materials. Maybe you have heard your child mention working with the golden beads, stamp game, bead frame, or checkerboard. Dr. Montessori intended these materials "to assist the children in learning to think and reason logically".

 

In this presentation we will discuss the benefits of learning with the Montessori math materials, which are three dimensional and multi-sensory. We will touch on research that shows how using these materials promote the proliferation of neural connections in the brain. Materials showing the mathematics sequence from counting, memorization of facts, the four basic operations, common and decimal fractions will be demonstrated. Finally, you will have the opportunity to handle those mysterious Montessori math materials yourself. Mark your calendar, because you will not want to miss this evening!

UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS

We are completing our study of ancient Greece this week. Students are looking at Greek numerals and finding that they use many of the same symbols as the letters. They are trying out the Sieve of Eratosthenes to identify prime numbers and following in the footsteps of Pythagoras exploring squares of numbers. We made clay amphoras, vessels used to carry water, wine, or olive oil. This Friday, we will celebrate with an ancient Greece festival, Panathenia. Students are making chitons and jewelry to wear and will enjoy some Greek snacks. UE parents, your child has volunteered you to bring in a food for this. SAPMS CAPITAL CAMPAIGN
Thank you to everyone that has already returned their Capital Campaign envelopes with your very generous donations. If you have not already done so, please return your donation and envelope by this Friday if possible to the school.

Due to the high front end cost of starting a new school (purchasing furniture, equipment, teaching materials, rent, building modifications, etc.), and not being eligible for capital outlay funds until the 4th year, fundraising is critical to any new Charter School (especially during the first four years).   For the last two years, SAPMS parents have hosted a large scale silent and live auction Gala which has raised the majority of the extra needed money each year. As you can imagine, this has been a huge undertaking, which has required a large time commitment from parents for the better part of over 6 months leading up to the Gala.
 
With SAPMS being such a small school, this magnitude of fundraiser is hard to sustain each year. Based on this and feedback from parents, it has been determined that we would do a Capital Campaign as our largest fundraiser in lieu of the Gala this year.Therefore, instead of dedicating large amounts of time planning and executing a Gala, parents can spend this valuable time with their most precious resource...their families! 
 
Thank you again for turning in your donations by Friday, and helping to make this Capital Campaign a big success so our children can continue to benefit from this wonderful Montessori education!
