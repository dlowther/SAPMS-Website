BUILDING THE MATHEMATICAL MIND

In the Montessori math curriculum the focus is not on the answer, but how the child gets the answer, promoting understanding, and learning how to think with precision and logic. This is what Montessori called the development of the mathematical mind. The materials are the key to understanding the process. The process comes first and then the memorization. The materials promote active rather than passive learning. Children make discoveries by manipulating the materials. They are more drawn to activities that ask them to figure out something for themselves.

 

Montessori math promotes brain development. The brain gets smarter by growing more synaptic connections between brain cells. We know that different parts of the brain perform different functions. Montessori math engages multiple brain areas including the four lobes of the cerebellum.

  • Occipital lobe (vision) is stimulated by the colors and shapes.
  • Temporal lobe (memory and hearing) takes in auditory cues and triggers memory association.
  • Parietal lobe (movement) is stimulated by the purposeful movement.
  • Frontal lobe (judgment, planning) is activated by the problem solving process needed to put all the sensory input together.

The activation of all four lobes creates neural connections.   The prefrontal lobe (seat of advanced thinking) is stimulated by active discovery learning, the repeated experiences, and the recognition of patterns. These all lead to AHA moments that light up the frontal lobe.

 

"Surprisingly, it doesn't matter to the brain whether it ever comes up with an answer. The neural growth happens because of the process, not the solution."   Eric Jensen, Teaching with the Brain in Mind.
