FALL FESTIVAL

Everyone had a great time at the Fall Festival last Friday. Ms. Lorraine's "twin sister", Insane Lorraine presented an entertaining science lesson on air pressure and states of matter. We had fun with a solid (dry ice) that went directly from a solid to a gas (sublimation). The UE percussion group, the Right Angle Triangles, gave a surprise performance. Ms. Heather and UE girls painted faces, and everyone enjoyed games like eyeball balance, spider races, pin the nose on the pumpkin, and bean bag toss. Thanks to Minnette Lusk and Tom Costeria for providing games for this event. Apple cider and popcorn were available for a donation to UNICEF.
UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS:

Students spent last Friday morning putting in a winter garden. Thanks to Dave Lowther who helped the students clear the soil, construct the raised bed frames and fill them with soil. We planted broccoli, cabbage, brussel sprouts, lettuce, carrots, and radishes. Can't wait for the harvest!

 

In our study of ancient Rome we've learned that the Romans were skilled builders with many of  their temples, theaters, bridges, and aqueducts still standing today. The Roman's use of concrete and the arch allowed them to build large structures like the Colosseum in Rome. Everyone has enjoyed constructing a model of the Roman arch and testing their engineering skills

LOGGERHEAD NEWS:  
In the Loggerhead class, students have been learning the three states of matter and how
molecules react. They have worked diligently on spider anatomy and human bones. We have also been working on writing Narratives using Onomatopoeia's, learning our math facts and are studying Europe and its countries. If anyone would like to help fill our classroom Country basket with teachable items about Italy, it would be greatly appreciated. We are looking for books; brochures and items children can taste and touch. We will need the items by November 18th.
DOLPHIN NEWS:    

This week the children are continuing their research of European countries and flags.  The children are focusing on practicing operations with materials and working with fractions. We are starting our study of horizontal, vertical, perpendicular, and intersecting lines.  We began the introduction to our solar system with a "dance of the planets" impressionistic lesson. This will lay the foundation for a series of experiments, research, and creative projects.

SAPMS CAPITAL CAMPAIGN - PHASE 2: Outreach
Thank you for the very generous donations for our Capital Campaign.  We have had a great response with 53% of our families participating so far donating close to $20K!   It is never too late, so if you have not had a chance to get your blue donation envelope in, you can drop it off to the school at any time.

 

We are now on to Phase 2 of the Capital Campaign, which is outreach to friends, family, and businesses for donations.   Jean previously sent an email with a template donation request letter and email template. Click here to view the templates: Template Solicitation Letter B 2013, Template General Fundraising Letter B 2013. If each family can reach out to 10 people, what a difference this would make for our school! 

 

Please personalize the letter and email it, send it, or personally deliver it to family, friends, and businesses you frequent.  For those that would rather do a Facebook blast and receive donations via PayPal, you can use this link to our SAPMS Donation page where people can donate to the school through PayPal: http://www.staugustinemontessori.com/support.htm.  Many of us reached out to numerous people last year to make donations to the Gala. Over 200 businesss donated silent auction items to the Gala last year along with lots of family and friends buying Gala tickets and raffle tickets. We can reach out to the same people explaining that we are requesting a small donation in lieu of silent auction items or tickets this year.  All donations are tax deductible.  Even if it is just $25 a person or business, this adds up fast!

 

A Google Docs spreadsheet has been created that everyone can access.  Click here for the link. This spreadsheet contains a master list of those that were solicited for silent auction donations and sponsorships for the Gala along with their contact information.  If you plan on reaching out to one of these businesses, simply type your name by the business in the "SAPMS Contact" column.  This way, anyone else accessing the spreadsheet will know not to contact businesses that already have a name in the SAPMS Contact Column.

 

If you want to contact someone not on the list, you can add them on the next line of the spreadsheet along with your name in the SAPMS Contact for that person/business.  Putting their contact information on the spreadsheet is optional, but doing so will ensure they receive a thank you letter for their tax records if they donate. 

 

Once you receive a donation from a person or business, please enter the amount of their donation in the "Donation Value" column.  This will be important for the thank you letter they receive for tax purposes.  The goal is to have all donations from this Phase 2 Outreach in before we go on Winter Break in December - great timing for those much needed end of year tax deductions! 

 

Thank you for helping with this second phase of our Capital Campaign!
