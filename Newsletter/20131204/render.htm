CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Whenever you have groups of children together, there are going to be conflicts. Unkind words are used and feelings are hurt. In the Montessori classroom instead of punishing misbehavior we help the children come together to discuss their problems. We use a format where the children face one another and take turns telling their version of the problem and how it made them feel. The other person listens and reflects back what he or she heard. Each child is encouraged to take responsibility for their part and say what they want from the other person. Possible solutions are discussed and one is agreed upon by both parties. The process is completed with a handshake and agreement to move forward without holding any grudges.


Sometimes children are angry and need time to cool off before beginning this process. In that case, we immediately stop the hurtful action, but avoid judging the fight. We show ways to discharge feelings acceptably and set limits and help them realize the futility of fighting. We begin the conflict resolution process in a more neutral moment. Like any other mistake in the classroom, these social mistakes are viewed as learning opportunities.


It is not possible for the adults in the environment to see and hear every hurtful interaction. We encourage children to let us know when there is a problem. These things are best handled at the time they occur. If children are complaining of a problem at home, ask if they talked to their teacher about it. If the answer is no, encourage them to address their concern the next day. If talking seems overwhelming, help them write a note. Our goal is to create cooperative classrooms where children learn to treat one another with respect.
