ONGOING SAGA

We had a long morning yesterday, as we checked the head of every student in an effort to stop the cycle of head lice infestation. We do not have a school nurse, so the teachers had to do this. We are doing the best we can with this and are sorry for any inconvenience caused by a mistaken call. Given the ongoing nature of this problem, it seems prudent to err on the side of caution.   I am looking into bringing training on identifying and dealing with lice to the school. Once this is arranged, parents will be invited to attend.

 

As has been stated previously, the key to eradication of this pest is the ongoing daily "nit picking". The hair must be combed daily with a special comb for ten days to two weeks to ensure removal of all nits. If all the nits are not removed, the cycle starts again. I have been told that there is a higher than average incidence of head lice in this area right now. Here's one more website with FAQs and photos.  http://lancaster.unl.edu/pest/lice/faq.shtml
