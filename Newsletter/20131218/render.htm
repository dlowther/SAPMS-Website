WINTER BREAK

We are hoping to break free of the head lice cycle over the break. The school will be cleaned during that time. Since lice can only live off their human host for a day or two at the most, there is nothing to be gained from applying pesticides to the environment.   We need every parent to be vigilant about checking for lice and nits over the break. Please check your child's head every day or two. Act immediately on any nits or lice found. Remember the shampoo only kills live lice. It will not kill nits or prevent infestation. Any nits not removed, will hatch out with new lice in about ten days.  Retreatment will then be necessary. I have a very detailed five page handout written for parents; please let me know if you would like a copy. We look forward to starting the new year with this problem behind us.

UPPER ELEMENTARY TRIP TO WHITNEY LAB

Our class enjoyed a fantastic day at the Whitney Lab last week. Whitney Lab for Marine Bioscience is a research institute for the University of Florida. While there, the students circulated through four activities that promoted observation and questioning. In one session students got to handle lobsters, conchs, urchins, sea stars, and horseshoe crabs. In another, they used microscopes to observe microscopic and planktonic organisms collected from the Whitney docks. They had to use the scientific method to conduct experiments in the other two activities. In one they collected data on the eating preferences of the mud snail, and tested density of fresh and salt water in the other. The day flew by for our student scientists.
