MONTESSORI EDUCATION ACTIVITY - THE JENGA PARADIGM

In this activity the game, Jenga, is used as a metaphor for the integrity and strength of  Montessori education. One essential element of Montessori education was written on each block. When taken together they create a structure with strong organizational integrity. Tinkering with the Montessori essentials may seem innocuous or even an improvement to the program. But one change can lead to another until authenticity is lost and the structure collapses. A few of the essential qualities included imagination, the prepared environment, independence, interdisciplinary connections, cosmic education, and cooperation & collaboration. After the activity, parents had a chance to vote for the top three qualities most important to them. Here are the top choices.

  • Hands on experiential learning
  • Peace education
  • Responsibility and self discipline
  • Freedom of movement
