NATIONAL SCHOOL CHOICE WEEK

January 26 - February 1 is National School Choice week.  This is a time to highlight the innovative educational options offered outside the traditional public school system.  SAPMS was founded by parents interested in providing a tuition free Montessori education to the children and families of St. John's County. Their efforts have provided a viable educational alternative in St. Augustine.   Our school is one of 25 public Montessori schools in Florida.  Thank you for choosing SAPMS for your child's education. 
PARENT VISITING DAY
Thanks to all who visited yesterday afternoon. The children look forward to this opportunity to show their work. The parents enjoy the chance to see those mysterious Montessori materials in action and get many questions answered.  How do you do long division with test tubes, long multiplication on a checkerboard or use a box of sticks to learn geometry? When parents participate in the life of the school, children get a sense of being part of an extended community, and we all benefit from this. We will have another visiting day the end of next month.

UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS 

Students are making the transition from Europe to Asia via the Silk Road.  The Silk Road was a 7,000 mile trade route that linked traders, merchants, pilgrims, monks, soldiers, and nomads from China to the Mediterranean Sea.  It got its name from the Chinese silk trade and opened political, economic, and cultural interactions between civilizations.  Do you have any Asian artifacts at home?  We would enjoy borrowing them for a while to display in the classroom. Our current math work includes decimals, angle measurement, calculating surface area, and measurement.  Language arts work is focused on writing five paragraph essays, using transition words, grammar and reading with our new SRA Reading Lab. In science we are examining physical and chemical changes. Finally, UE students have begun using an individual daily planner in addition to the weekly group work plan.  Each piece of work successfully completed is initialed by me.  Students are bringing these home on Fridays.  Please look for them and use them to discuss your child's week at school.
