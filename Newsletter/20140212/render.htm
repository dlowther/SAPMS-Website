UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS 

Students completed a collaborative project about the Silk Road. Each student found photos to represent the various goods traded along the Silk Road. These were mounted on a map giving a strong visual of this historically important trade route. After honing their expository writing skills, students are working on narrative writing this week. As with our other writing they will focus on using vivid descriptions, precise language, and transition words. In math we are practicing long division and long multiplication skills, simplifying fractions, squaring two and three digit numbers, with lessons on integers and Hands on Equations.   Everyone is learning the parts of our new microscope and looking forward to seeing the world up close and personal. We are looking to our walk to the Mission on Friday to participate in the Great Backyard Bird Count. We have been participating in Project Feeder Watch and find we have quite a few regulars at our feeder.
CHRONIC ABSENTEEISM
In many school districts, including St. John's County, chronic absenteeism is defined as missing 10% or more of the school year. That translates to 18 or more days missed. Last year over 50% of our students missed this amount of school. Many studies have shown that regular school attendance is related to higher academic achievement. When your child is not in school, he or she misses lessons and practice with previously taught skills. Children miss the intellectual stimulation of interacting with their peers in the collaborative Montessori learning environment. Missing one day in a week means missing 20% of available learning time. Obviously, we do not want you to send sick children to school. However, we do ask that you reconsider keeping your child away from school for recreational activities. Regular school attendance is an important factor in school success.
