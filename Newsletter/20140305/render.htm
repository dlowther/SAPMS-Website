GOOD NUTRITION AND SCHOOL ACHIEVEMENT 
We all want our children to do well in school, and providing a nutritious breakfast and lunch is a sure way to facilitate that. Numerous studies have shown that good nutrition is important for optimal brain functioning. Meals that are mainly comprised of sugar and starch cause a drop in your child's blood sugar leaving him or her tired and unfocused.   To help your children be ready to learn feed them a balanced diet of nutrient dense foods. Proteins are the building blocks of neurotransmitters in the brain and provide sustained energy. These can include lean meats, low fat dairy products, eggs, beans, and unsalted nuts and seeds. Fresh fruits provide antioxidants and help boost immunity levels. Fresh vegetables provide vitamins, minerals, and are high in fiber and low in calories. Limit carbohydrates to less processed products made with whole grains. Aim to limit empty calories from chips and other highly processed snack foods as well as sugary treats. Healthful nutritional habits learned early in life develop positive healthy eating habits to support optimal brain functioning.
