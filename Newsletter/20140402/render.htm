SAPMS MISSION STATEMENT

Our mission is to provide students in Grades 1 through 6 (ages 6-12) with a learning culture that is grounded in Montessori philosophy. This historically proven educational model supports the whole child, creates lifelong learners, and educates for collaborative approaches to community leadership. The foundation of this academic excellence is a prepared classroom environment that inspires self-paced, individualized discovery; a love of learning; and respect for self, others, and the environment.

 

Education is a Journey not a Race

 In the past, the goals of education were tied to the goals of a democratic society: to prepare people to become responsible citizens, improve social conditions, promote cultural unity, and help people become economically self-sufficient.  Federal funding was used to provide equity by distributing federal dollars to schools and districts that enrolled students with the highest needs.  Today Federal education policy, Race to the Top, has done away with this equity.  Education has become a competition, with highest performing schools receiving the most money.  High standardized test scores have somehow become the measure of quality education.  Students are expected to increase their test scores every year to be considered proficient. High stakes testing, accountability, and data driven instruction are the hallmarks of current educational "reform".  In addition to test scores, states collect tremendous amounts of data on students including name, address, birth date, social security numbers, grades, disability status, and ethnicity.  States receive federal funding to create data warehouses to store information on students and teachers.

 

Teaching to the test was once considered unethical.  Now it is common practice or even expected.  While we (at SAPMS) are all ensuring that standards are being covered and students know how to approach a standardized test, we do not devote a large percentage of our class time teaching to the test.  This emphasis on standardized tests does not prepare children for the real world.  Real world skills like the ability to synthesize information or collaboration are not measured on tests.  Tests do no measure character, spirit, heart, soul, or potential.

From Kate...

"There are moments in our kids' lives that we think we are going to remember forever. But time goes on and our kids get older. That favorite toy or blanket that our children couldn't leave home without gets forgotten under the bed or in the closet. This project is to make a mark in your memory (and some day theirs) of whatever means the most to your kiddo right now. My goal with this art project is to see the light in their eyes when they talk about their favorite thing or their quiet spirit as they shyly show me their trinket.
