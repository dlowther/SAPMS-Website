CLASS / COMMUNITY MEETING

It was great to see so many families make it to the Class and Community Meeting last night.  Following are a few highlights in case you missed it.  Board member, Carrie Morgan, has completed her three year term, so we are seeking another parent board member.  This is your chance to have input into the decision making process for our school.  As several board members pointed out last night, this is a working board with many hours required beyond the monthly two hour board meetings.  If you would like an opportunity to serve your school as a board member, please contact Jean McDowel at margaret.jean.mcdowell@gmail.com   


 

 Everyone enjoyed seeing the new modular classroom.  This spacious layout is what we want for all of our students.  Unfortunately, the dollars we receive from the state do not even begin to fund the type of facility we need.  This will require a mighty fundraising effort by our stakeholders including participation in an Annual Fund.  You will hear more about our Annual Fund goals in the coming weeks.


 "The goal of education is the development of a complete human being, oriented to the environment and adapted to his or her time, place, and culture."  Maria Montessori


 Maria Montessori had a vision of a new sort of education.  After sharing her goal for education, I asked the parents present to express their goal for their child's education.  I will share a few of these below and more in future newsletters.


 "My goal for my child is for him to take ownership of his learning and to love learning as well as for him to be a confident and compassionate person of good character."


 "My goal is for my child to become self-sufficient and develop a passion for lifelong learning."


 "Our goal is for our children to develop into creative, open minded, sensitive, compassionate, self-directed individuals who love learning."


 If you did not attend the meeting, I invite you to think about your goal for your child's education.  I would enjoy hearing it, if you would like to share.

DISMISSAL CAR LINE

Please do not enter the car line more than five minutes before dismissal.  LE dismissal starts a quarter before the hour and UE dismissal is on the hour. Entering the car line 15, 20, or 30 minutes before dismissal causes a back up and delay for everyone. Thank you for your attention to this. 
LICE PREVENTION

We ask that every parent check their child's head for the presence of nits and lice on a weekly basis. If you find any, please keep your child home and seek treatment.  A detailed information sheet on how to do this went home in your child's folder earlier this week.  Let Gina in the office know if you did not receive this and we will get one to you. Following are websites that will tell you more than you ever wanted to know about head lice along with color photos, so you know what you are looking for. www.Headlice.org and www.cdc.gov/parasites/lice/head/treatment.html
