FROM THE BOARD

In this and upcoming newsletters, you will hear from the SAPMS Board of Trustees.  Following is a copy of a talk given by board treasurer, Nan Nutt at our first Community Meeting. 


I am Nan Nutt, board treasurer for the SAPMS.  I've been doing this for just a year.  I really want this school to succeed. We are still in our infancy and we need your help to make sure we thrive and grow.


 

Why am I doing my part on the board? 

  • It's not for the money I get - I am a volunteer like the rest of the board
  • I have no children or grandchildren to send to the school
  • I do not own part of some school equipment and supply company
  • I have only
    • a love of learning,
    • an idea from my own teaching experience about what works
    • and real-life Montessori success stories in my own family. 

I expect some of these motivations are similar to yours.


I am passionate about the idea that all children should have the chance to find their passions as they explore and learn about the world.  They should be the ones who drive the process, not the state, not the district, not the board, not the teachers, not even you, the parents. I believe that this is what the Montessori Method is all about.  I am delighted that St Augustine can offer a public, tuition-free Montessori education.  What an opportunity!


Of course, surrounding this educational model are the usual demands of making an institution work.  I have spent my first year working with Judi and the other board members to understand the school budget, track it and deal with the various entities- our bookkeeper, banks, vendors, the district, auditors, and so on - all who have an impact on the money we have and how we spend it.


Along with Judi and the rest of the board, I worry about providing the resources the school and staff needed to provide an authentic Montessori environment.   We work with a very tight operating budget, in fact a budget that requires private as well as district funding. 


So now I am at the bottom line -- we need your help to make sure we grow and thrive.  We need your time, your talents, and your treasure.


What can you offer to help make sure that the education you want for your child is the education they get?  Some of you have already offered a great deal.  One group in particular I want to acknowledge - the volunteers who helped get the modular ready for the beginning of school.  It was an amazing effort made especially challenging by vendor delays, lack of careful planning, and inadequate oversight.  The board is well aware of the difficulties you encountered and we apologize for that.  We thank you all for the significant time, talent, and treasure you donated to this project!  I hope everyone appreciates the wonderful space this modular provides the school and the service that you provided.  Special thanks to Carole Loughlin for her coordination efforts.


And now the next challenge: Beyond the donations already made to get the modular in place, we must raise approximately 12% of our operating budget from private donations.  A major source of donations comes from our annual fund.  In next several weeks, you will receive a mailing with specific information about the annual fund, including suggestions about enlisting others to make donations.  Please read it carefully and consider your contribution.  In fact, as I believe, these donations are simply part and parcel of a Montessori education. 


Your children will remember what you do for their school and for them. Please join us in our effort to make sure that the education you want for your children is the education they get!

VIGILANCE

We had a nurse check all heads a week ago.  She found nothing at that time, but that does not mean that you can stop checking.  A school head check only tells us that at that point in time we found no lice or nits.  Over the weekend we had one family report finding a couple lice and nits in the child's hair.  You are the front line here.  It is imperative that all parents check their own child's head on a regular basis.  You will give much more time to it that we can in a mass screening.  We will continue to check if a child is scratching or shows other possible signs of lice and notify you if anything is found.  Continue to keep long hair pulled back and use your oils around the hairline. If you do find something, treat immediately and keep us informed. 
UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS 

Students enjoyed Ms. Kirby's Big Bang presentation.  As a follow-up younger students will receive an introduction to the Periodic Table.  Older students will complete a short research paper on the chemical element of their choice.  Fourth year students are working on common and proper nouns and the rules for capitalization using these.  They are also using the Montessori grammar symbols to identify each part of speech in a short selection of literature.  Older students are learning about the power of the number three in writing and writing interpretative questions in response to literature. Math work includes long multiplication and division, dividing fractions, and balancing algebraic equations along with weekly word problems.
