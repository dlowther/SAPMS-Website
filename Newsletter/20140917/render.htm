INTERNATIONAL DAY OF PEACE CELEBRATION

Each year the International Day of Peace is observed around the world on September 21.  This day was established by a United Nations resolution and the first Peace Day was observed in 1982.  The United Nations invites all nations and people to honor a cessation of hostilities during the day and to commemorate the day with education and public awareness on issues related to peace. 


We all know that Maria Montessori was an innovative educator. However her true desire was to reform humanity in order to bring peace and harmony to the world.  She thought it strange that there was no science of peace.  In Peace and Education she wrote, "If education recognizes the intrinsic value of the child's personality and provides an environment suitable to spiritual growth, we have a revelation of an entirely new child, whose astonishing characteristics can eventually contribute to the betterment of the world."  One of Montessori's contemporaries, Mohandas Gandhi said, "If we are to teach real peace in this world, and if we are to carry on a real war against war, we shall have to begin with the children.


Upper elementary students are participating in the Peace Crane Project, which was designed to promote the concept of peace and community within and among our schools, homes, states, and nations.  Children around the world will exchange peace cranes with one another.  Our class will exchange our cranes with children in a school in Australia.  You will have an opportunity to make a peace crane and sign the Peace Pledge at our celebration on Friday.


We will hold a Peace March at 10:30 this Friday.  Families are invited to join us.  Please wear white.  Students must be wearing closed toe shoes, preferably sneakers to participate in the march.
ARRIVAL AND DISMISSAL 

All students must be dropped off no later than 8:25, so they are in their classrooms and ready to begin their day at 8:30. Lower elementary students' arrival window is 8:05 - 8:15. Upper elementary window is 8:15 - 8:25.  This morning there were many LE parents in the car line during the UE window.  This makes it difficult for UE students to get into their classroom on time. We ask that you please observe the time scheduled for you child's class. 


 

 When students are consistently late, their late arrival is disruptive to the classroom.  They miss the morning greetings and any instructions for the day that may have been given to the group. Their friends have all settled into work alone or with a friend and the late child is left at a disadvantage.  Please get your child to school on time.


 Dismissal times were staggered this year to accommodate increased enrollment.  I have noticed that more and more Lower Elementary parents are coming after 3:00.  All LE students who do not travel with Upper Elementary students should be picked up no later than 3:00.  Lower Elementary students go out to the deck by 2:45.  When you do not arrive until 3:10, they have been sitting and waiting for 25 minutes.   Thank you for your attention to this.
