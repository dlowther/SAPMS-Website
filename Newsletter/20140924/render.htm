PEACE DAY FOLLOW UP

Peace education is a huge aspect of Montessori education. So it was fitting that this was our first big school wide celebration. In the everyday life of the classroom, peace is promoted in small but significant ways.  The teachers model respect, empathy, and consideration for others, as well as facilitating and encouraging nonviolent approaches to conflict resolution.   Our classrooms place a strong emphasis on collaboration and cooperation.  We allow learners to have real responsibilities for helping one another. Our Peace March to the Mission last week allowed our students and accompanying parents the opportunity to join their intentions with people from around the world in support of a culture of world peace.  Peace is an amazing concept, and we hope it is an attainable goal in the lifetime of our students.  It is certainly well worth celebrating. 
ENRICHMENT CLASSES 

Parent volunteer, Minette Lusk is teaching PE to both lower and upper elementary classes.  Look for the unveiling of our new fitness initiative, Montessori Moves.

 

This Thursday, music will return for all classes.  Ms. Laura Antoni-Beard is our new teacher.  Laura comes with years of experience teaching music at all age levels.  We are excited to welcome her to our school.

 

This year's Studio Program is starting on Friday.  Studios are short (2 week) enrichment classes taught by our staff or parent volunteers.  This year we have a new model with a two week studio followed by a week off.  Students are able to sign up for studios, and students from different classes are grouped together.

 

Please contact me if you would like to teach a studio.  You will be able to pick the age group you would like to work with and the number of students in your group.  The kids are interested in all sorts of things.  If you are enthusiastic about your offering, they will be too.
