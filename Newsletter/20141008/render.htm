INDEPENDENCE AND RESPONSIBILITY

The Montessori classroom is a community of learners.  It is a place of independence and responsibility.  Children are expected to manage their own time, choose their own activities, and move freely about the classroom without disturbing others.  They are expected to interact in a respectful manner with both their peers and adults.  In order to do this, children must develop maturity and responsibility.  To be a successful learner in the Montessori classroom, children must have the ability to self-regulate their behavior, demonstrate self- motivation, and have an interest in learning.


In our classrooms very little time is devoted to whole class instruction.  Instead, each teacher carefully designs a learning environment that contains activities uniquely suited to the children's interests and developmental abilities.  Children are free choose their activities within the guidelines set by the teacher.  They are required to finish all projects they start and return all materials to their proper place and in good condition.  As the children mature and develop they become responsible for more tasks.  There is a dual emphasis on increasing levels of independence and responsibility.

By giving your child increasing responsibilities and independence at home, you can help increase your child's sense of intrinsic discipline and self-control.

  • Allow your child to manage their own take home folders, lunch boxes, and jackets.  Provide a defined space and consistent routine for this.
  • Give your child household chores, like helping set and clear the table, keeping their room neat, and any others that work for your family.
  • Help your child appreciate the value of their belongings by requiring that they are used for the intended purpose and cared for properly.

UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS
The students enjoyed their field trip to the Guana Tolomato Preserve.  The focus of the field trip was to study the hazardous effects of marine debris in Florida's aquatic systems and learn about North Florida's estuaries and the importance they have on the natural environment.  The students did a beach clean up and made scientific charts of the debris. They then went into the wonderful exhibit at the environmental research center and did a scavenger hunt to find some of Florida's species of plants and animals found in the estuaries and shorelines. The day was wrapped up with a picnic lunch and we even had time to stop at the county park off 24th street for play time amongst the beautiful oak trees!

LOWER ELEMENTARY NEWS
Lower Elementary students enjoyed our in school field trip with the Whitney Labs traveling zoo.  Students learned how starfish regenerate their limbs, touched the sticky anemones tentacles and learned differences of crabs.  They also learned the differences between Invertebrates and Vertebrates.  Each class is working on the Phases of the Moon and studying the planets of our solar system.  Third years have a planet project due on October 17th.  Please see your child's folder for details.  We also had our first two weeks of studios where children enjoyed knitting, nature art and science of Legos.
