NO SCHOOL FRIDAY

As shown on your school calendar, SAPMS will be closed this Friday, November 7, although other district schools will be open.  Ms. Heather, Ms. Kirby, Ms. Lorraine and I will be in Sarasota to attend the 18th Annual International Conference of the Montessori Foundation.  There we will hear inspiring keynote speakers and attend workshops that will help us refresh knowledge and deepen our expertise as Montessori educators.  We expect to return filled with great ideas that will positively impact our school community.


 School will also be closed the following Monday, November 10 in observance of Veteran's Day.

 
ANNUAL FUND

Thank you to all who have returned their donations and pledges.  We appreciate you!  Our school is seriously underfunded, as SAPMS does not receive funding comparable to other district schools.  We count on support from our school family and friends to help us provide the high quality Montessori education we all want for the children.  As we seek other funding from outside sources, it is important for us to be able to say that 100% of our school community supports us.  If you cannot afford the suggested donation, please give what you can afford.  Small donations help us reach that 100% goal.  If you can afford to give more, please do that.  Remember, your children are receiving a private school education for free!

 
EQUAL EXCHANGE

I hope you have had time to look at the Equal Exchange catalog that was sent home.  This fundraising is just a win-win all the way around.  The organic and fairly traded chocolates, coffees, and teas are the best you can buy.  Our purchases support a company that gives third world farmers a fair price for their products, while encouraging sustainable agriculture.  The nonfood items come from Ten Thousand Villages, which is a well- known fair trade retailer.  I especially enjoy the tree free wrapping paper.  Made from cotton, this wrapping paper will elicit wows before the gift is even opened!  Best of all, our school can earn a 40% profit on all items purchased.  What are you waiting for?  Please return your order form and check made out to SAPMS by November 11.

UNICEF

If your child chose to collect donations for UNICEF, please return the orange boxes to school.  For decades UNICEF has been a leader in providing programs to children in need around the world.  91 cents of every dollar the US Fund for UNICEF receives goes directly to programs that help children.  We have encouraged students to donate some of their own money.  Our children are so blessed.  It is a wonderful thing for them to be able to contribute to children who have so much less.
