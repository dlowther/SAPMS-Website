MONTESSORI CONFERENCE

We thoroughly enjoyed our time at the Montessori conference last week.  We were able to attend a variety of Montessori workshops and enjoy several keynote speakers.  Meals were shared communally giving us an opportunity to connect with Montessori educators from around the country and world.  People came from as far away as Alaska and Nigeria.  Keynote speaker, Dr. Paul Epstein, reminded us that many aspects of Montessori education which we view as ordinary are really seen as quite extraordinary outside of Montessori.  These would include the order, beauty, independence, and self-regulation found in a normalized Montessori classroom.  Another keynote speaker, Dr. Laura Bakosh, reminded us of the detrimental effect of screen time (TV, video games, Internet) on children's brain development. Screen time causes atrophy in the brain's frontal lobe, which governs executive functioning, planning, prioritizing, and impulse control. It is frontal lobe development that largely determines success in all areas of life - from a sense of well- being, to academic, career, and relationship success.  We also attended a variety of workshops on peace education, conflict resolution, geometry, leadership, classroom observation, and children with learning differences.   We are most appreciative of this professional development and grateful to our Board for approving this expense.

BUTTERFLIES

Now that our cassia tree is blooming we have had beautiful sulphur butterflies fluttering around and laying eggs.  We watched the caterpillars hatch and feast on the leaves.  Upper elementary students are now eagerly awaiting that miraculous transformation of chrysalis to adult butterfly.
