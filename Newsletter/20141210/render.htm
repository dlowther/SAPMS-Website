WINTER FESTIVAL

Our students are preparing for the debut performance of A Stork in a Baobab Tree, An African Twelve Days of Christmas.  This version of the traditional Christmas song compliments our African studies.  Each of the days highlights a custom from a different African nation. Our festival begins at 6:00 and will include other short performances.  This will be followed by a pot luck dinner.  We've always had a great assortment of delicious dishes and I'm sure this year will be no different.  We ask that everyone bring their own set-up for dinner, plates, cutlery, and cups.  Extended family and friends are welcome to attend.  Picnic table seating is limited, so bring your own chair and dress for the weather; this is an outdoor event.

 

Look for a note coming home regarding what your child may need for their part in the song.

UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS

Even with the approaching holidays, our UE students continue to work hard.  Math work includes percentages, addition and subtraction of integers, factors of numbers, prime and composite numbers, and long division.  Younger students are completing African animal research, while older students are taking notes on an African country.  Their final product will be a brochure. Students are also reviewing types of nouns, common/proper, abstract, and collective.  Older students continue their studies of early humans learning about Homo Erectus, as younger students are studying rocks and minerals. 

 
UNICEF Update

The children collected $257 for UNICEF. This money will go to help children in need around the world.

VOLUNTEER SERVICE HOURS

Your Contract of Commitment requires that you give 20 hours of service to the school each year.  At this point we have a number of families who have exceeded this requirement and continue to give of their time.  We thank you for that.  We also have families who have 10 or fewer service hours logged.  I will be notifying those families who are less than half way to the required hours.  We also have families who have not entered their hours in the volunteer log.  If this is your situation, please stop in the office and update your hours.  The binder is on a shelf right inside the front door.  Your child's seamless enrollment next year is contingent on your completion of these hours.  Visit the Volunteer Spot to see what you can do.
