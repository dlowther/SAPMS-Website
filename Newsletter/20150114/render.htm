PROGRESS REPORTS

Your child's first written evaluation will come home on Friday. For those of you new to Montessori, this report probably looks very different than what you have been used to seeing.   Students are evaluated individually, and we look at the whole child.  Social development and practical life skills (work habits) are assessed along with mastery of fundamental skills.  Please take the time to read it carefully. You may want to discuss the Practical Life Skills and Social Skills sections with your child.  Contact your child's teacher if you have any questions. Another form of assessment used is the students' portfolio of their work.  As listed on your special events calendar, we are having a Portfolio Afternoon tomorrow, Thursday, January 15 from 2:30 - 3:30.  This is your opportunity to visit the classroom and have your child show you their work.  We hope you can make it.

AFRICA FESTIVAL
I'd like to thank the Holbrook family for lending our classes many authentic African artifacts. The children enjoyed David's (Juan's dad) presentation on his travels in Tanzania. It brought our studies to life. 

 

The culminating activity of our Africa studies was a great success.  Each classroom chose an African country to highlight, then prepared a game, a food, and a craft representative of it. The children researched different aspects of their country and created a product.  They were engaged and proud to show their work. Thanks to everyone who joined us for this cultural festival.
