WHAT'S SPECIAL ABOUT SAPMS?

All of our families are here because you are seeking something different from what is offered in the traditional public school system. Following are some characteristics of Montessori education that set us apart from your neighborhood school.

  • Rather than focusing on test scores, the aim of Montessori education is to foster competent, adaptable citizens who are lifelong learners and problem solvers
  • Learning occurs in an inquiring, cooperative, nurturing atmosphere as opposed to a competitive classroom where students are ranked against one another
  • Classrooms that are communities of children and adults working and learning together
  • Respect and caring attitudes for oneself, others, the environment and all life are fostered
  • Learning is active - students have the opportunity to select work and make choices about how to organize their day
  • A classroom atmosphere that encourages social interaction with cooperative learning, peer teaching, and emotional development
  • A class schedule that allows for large uninterrupted blocks of time to problem solve, to see interdisciplinary connections of knowledge, and to create new ideas
  • A diverse set of Montessori materials, activities, and experiences which are designed to foster physical, intellectual, creative, and social independence
  • Provides opportunities for service  ranging from daily contributions to others within the class or school setting to community outreach programs
  • Promotes global awareness and universal values
We are proud to be able to offer this option to the children and families of St. Johns County.

 
PROGRESS REPORTS

You should have all received your child's first written progress report.  If you have not, please contact your child's teacher ASAP.  These reports are meant to cover the entire three year curriculum at each level, 6-9 and 9-12.  This is why there are skills and concepts that have not been evaluated on your child's report.  This will be especially true for our younger students.  We hope you find them informative and useful.  All teachers are available to answer any questions you might have.  Please set up an appointment to discuss these if needed.

 
COMMUNITY MEETING

These meetings are held three times each year.  They are for you.  They provide an opportunity to hear about what is going on at our school, express concerns, and ask questions.  You will hear from our Board of Directors concerning plans for next school year and our financial status.  We will also get an update from our FUNdraising Committee.  Your Contract of Commitment requires you to attend two of the three meetings, so if you missed the first one, you must attend this one.  We will open the meeting with a fun activity designed to get people moving and help us get to know one another a little better.  Be sure to get there on time! 

 

The meeting will be held in the modular classroom.  Childcare will be available in the upstairs multi-purpose room for a donation of $5/child.  Please RSVP to Ms. Gina or me, if you plan to use this. 


 

Parking -  Please be mindful about where you park for this meeting.  Our neighbors were unhappy the night of the Winter Festival.  ABLE has a small parking lot on the east side on San Marco just south of us.  There is also parking available in the CVS lot across from the ABLE lot.

SILENT AUCTION ACTION NEEDED!

With only 3 months to go, we have a theme, now we need lots of silent auction donations!  This is really where we need everyone to chip in and help! Silent auction donations is where 95% of the money will be raised from the gala for the school.    

 
We have made it easy to help.  First, we are mailing solicitation letters to over 350 local businesses this week.  We will make this list of businesses viewable to everyone.  You can choose businesses from this list you want to be charge of following up with after they receive the letters.  You can also ADD new businesses you want to ask.  The best business to ask are those that you frequent (ex. restaurants, salons, gyms, friends that work at local establishments, etc).  
 
Second, here is a link to the solicitation letter and form. Please print and take this with you as you patron local businesses and ask for their support through an in-kind donation. Feel free to personalize it and email it to businesses as well.  Businesses really are willing to donate items or services.  It is way easier than asking for straight money.  It is just a matter of following up with the businesses and taking the time to pick up items.
