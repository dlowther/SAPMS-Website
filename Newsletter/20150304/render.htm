TESTING SEASON

Our sixth grade students took the first FSA Writing test today.  Our fourth and fifth grade students will sit for this exam tomorrow.  Testing will pick up again after spring break when the third and fourth year students will take the English Language Arts and Mathematics assessments at the end of the month and start again in mid- April.  These standardized tests are meant to measure grade level proficiency months before the school year is over. The scores become part of the students' annual record and are pooled with other students' scores to measure performance in a given class, school, or school district and are often published in local newspapers.  Now the trend is to tie teacher pay to test scores, although we hear that will be suspended this year.  All of this tends to encourage teachers to focus on test preparation by teaching the skills that will be on the test.

 

In Montessori schools we think about the learning process and assessment in a different way.  We focus on the individual progress rather than ranking children against one another. Assessment is ongoing and is used to support learning instead of measuring it.  For us, assessment is informal and measures growth on a continuum of learning.  It provides useful feedback to both students and teachers. It helps students understand their current level of knowledge and provides a challenge for them to work toward mastery.  It helps the teacher know whether it is time to move on to the next lesson or reteach. 

 

High stakes standardized testing is a fact of life in public schools today.   While we do our best to cover the standards and prepare the children for the testing experience, our school's true mission is to provide an authentic Montessori education for our students.  In our first two years of testing, our students' scores ranged from 2 - 5(highest level) with most meeting proficiency.  We will never have the highest test scores in the district, but we will have students who have a broad knowledge base and love to learn.
