GOOD NUTRITION AND SCHOOL ACHIEVEMENT

We all want our children to do well in school, and providing a nutritious breakfast and lunch is a sure way to facilitate that.   Numerous studies have shown that good nutrition is important for optimal brain functioning.  Meals that are mainly comprised of sugar and starch cause a drop in your child's blood sugar leaving him or her tired and unfocused.   To help your children be ready to learn feed them a balanced diet of nutrient dense foods.  Proteins are the building blocks of neurotransmitters in the brain and provide sustained energy.  These can include lean meats, low fat dairy products, eggs, beans, and unsalted nuts and seeds.  Fresh fruits provide antioxidants and help boost immunity levels.  Fresh vegetables provide vitamins, minerals, and are high in fiber and low in calories.  Limit carbohydrates to less processed products made with whole grains.  Aim to limit empty calories from chips and other highly processed snack foods as well as sugary treats. Children who are used to eating a diet high in processed foods may resist the introduction of more healthy foods.  Try limiting the less healthy options to an occasional snack and fill your refrigerator and pantry with better nutritional options.  Healthful nutritional habits learned early in life develop positive healthy eating habits to support optimal brain functioning.

FIELD DAY

We have planned a great community event for our students this Thursday.  We will gather at Treaty Park, which is on Wildwood Drive.  Children will be divided into small teams.  Third year students will be grouped with Upper Elementary to help them start to get to know each other better.  Teams will move around stations to participate in relay races, three legged races, parachute games and more.  Cooperation will be stressed over competition.  We will eat lunch together under the pavilion.  After lunch students will have time to play on the playground there and walk on the boardwalk.  It promises to be lots of fun, so let's hope Mother Nature cooperates.  Students should wear their school t-shirts and athletic shoes.  Everyone needs to bring a lunch and water bottle. Due to predictions of afternoon rain, we will change our departure time to 9:30 and leave the park at 12:30.  We still need drivers. Please contact your child's teacher if you can help with this.

CHICKEN POX

We have had another case of chicken pox in Lower Elementary.   We all know about the spotty red rash that is associated with this disease.  But your child may experience headache, sore throat, and mild fever before the rash appears.  If you child exhibits any of these symptoms, please keep him or her home.  If they persist, see your doctor. Children who have had chicken pox must have a doctor's note to return to school.  You will receive a letter today from the health department regarding this
