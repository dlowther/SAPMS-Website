GALA

The big evening is only 18 days away, yet only a few more than half our families have purchased tickets.  Not only is this event an important fundraiser for our school, it's going to be a great night out.  You don't want to miss out on all the fun!  Tickets may be purchased online at our website, www.staugustinemontessori.com by clicking on Gala 2015, or you can bring check or cash to the school office.  This is a chance to socialize and become better acquainted with your Montessori school community.  Don't wait, get your tickets today!

FSA

Today is the last day of testing for third and fourth grade students.  We will continue with make-up testing next week.  Everything went smoothly with minimum stress for our students.  Thank you to Nancy Costeria and Amanda Westby for volunteering to proctor.


 Computer based testing for fifth and sixth grade students will pick up mid-April and continue through the end of the month.  We have a couple of volunteer proctors but will need more.  Please let me know if you can assist with this.  This is a very easy way to earn some service hours, if you are available during school hours.

 
UPPER ELEMENTARY NEWS

As mentioned above, computer based testing will begin in the middle of April for fifth and sixth grade students.  Each student will need to have headphones or ear buds for this and for the training tests to be conducted next week.  Please send in a pair for your child in a ziplock bag with their name clearly printed on it. We would like to be able to keep these at school.


 

During all this we are carrying on with our classwork.  Younger students are studying the Timeline of Life, while older students are researching early explorers to North America.  Next week we will begin to study the thirteen colonies and what happened between 1600 and 1740.  We will be using book 2 of Joy Hakim's History of US as our main text and focus on the themes change, diversity, and conflict and how they relate to this nation's quest for freedom. 


 

Students should have completed their science project reports and beworking on their display boards.  Many thanks to Jean McDowell for initiating and driving this project.  I can't wait to see the finished projects.  Our first Science Fair will be held on Thursday, April 9 from 4:30 - 6:30. Everyone is invited to come and see the great science work our kids have been doing.
