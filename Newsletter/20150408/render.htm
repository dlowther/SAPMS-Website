SCIENCE FAIR

Our first annual Science Fair will be held tomorrow afternoon, Thursday, April 9, from 4:30 - 6:00 in the modular classroom.  Everyone is invited to attend.  Students from third to sixth grade have been working hard on their investigations using the scientific method. They have produced a research report and a display board documenting each step of their project. During the school day, students will present their projects to judges who will ask questions and provide feedback.  Projects will not be ranked and no awards will be given. 

 

All students will be picked up at regular dismissal time and return to school at 4:30 for the fair. We hope to see a good turnout from our families of younger students.  This will be a great opportunity for your child to see what the older kids are doing and what's ahead for them.

OBELISK ART

This is a major public art project undertake by Compassionate St Augustine to pay tribute to the 450th anniversary of our city and promote a legacy of compassion.  This initiative aims to encourage citizens to follow and respect four values - Freedom, Democracy, Human Rights, and Compassion. The talents of 25 renowned national and international artists will be displayed on 8.5' obelisks.  Additionally, four foot obelisks were given to a number of area schools.  We were lucky to be chosen to participate in this project. We read stories about compassionate acts and had many discussions on what compassion looks like in our daily lives.  Students worked collaboratively to interpret and create a visual representation of compassion awareness.  Many thanks to Ms. Nancy for her invaluable help in the execution of this project.  The obelisks will be displayed around town, and I will inform you when I know the venues.
