SPROUTING SEEDS OF SUCCESS

The big party is this Saturday.  We want this to be a real community event where families and staff can relax and have fun together.  So if you haven't gotten your ticket, now's the time to do it!  Tickets may be purchased at the door, but please purchase in advance if possible.  This will enable us to give an accurate head count to the venue.  Tickets are $35 and include tapas, a drink, live music, and great company. The Colonial Quarter is an outdoor venue, and you will want to dress accordingly.  You'll be walking on mulch, so leave the heels at home and no ties. We're aiming for a dressy casual look.

 

Be sure check in when you arrive.  You'll pick up tickets ordered online there, and receive your drink ticket.  Hang onto your event ticket.  Your ticket number is what you will use to bid on items in the silent auction. To defray transaction charges from the credit card companies, there will be an additional 3% added to all credit card payments.  Bring a check or cash to pay for your purchases and save 3%.

 

We have had a core group who have been working hard for months.  They could really use some additional assistance on Saturday.  Go to Volunteer Spot (see below) and sign up to help with set up before event, various tasks during the event, or clean up after the event.  This will count toward service hours and be most appreciated. Also, I will give double service hours to anyone who stays to help with the clean up. 

 

It would be great if we could see all of our families there.  It will be an enjoyable evening where we can relax and let our hair down without the kids around.  I can't think of a better way for our Montessori community to become better acquainted.

 
SCIENCE FAIR

Our first Science Fair was a huge success.  Our young scientists conducted some great experiments and learned a lot in the process.  I learned a lot too, as the students explained their projects to me!  Once again, thanks to Jean McDowell for coordinating this and to all the parents who supported their children's effort, and to our judges who took the morning to discuss the students' work with them.
