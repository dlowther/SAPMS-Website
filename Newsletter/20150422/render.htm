SPROUTING SEEDS OF SUCCESS

If you weren't there Saturday night, you missed a great party! Many, many thanks to Leilanie Pascasio for organizing and leading the effort to put this all together. Thanks also to the many SAPMS parents who put in many long hours making this all happen and then stayed to take it down.  The rain was a little challenge early on, but no one let it put a damper on the evening.  The Silent Auction had so many great selections and thanks to our intrepid MCs for bringing the fun and excitement of a live auction for some of the items.  The live music was fabulous, thanks to Lonesome Bert and the Skinny Lizards, the food delicious and the ambience unbeatable.  Early reports indicate that we did very well.  We will let you know how well when we get the final numbers.  Thank you to everyone who participated in this successful evening.

 

THE HOME STRETCH:
Have You Fulfilled Your Contract of Commitment?

We are down to the final six weeks of school, but there are still opportunities to fulfill your Contract of Commitment.  As a reminder, each family agreed to the following terms at registration.

  • Attend New Parent Orientation
  • Attend at least 2 of 3 Community Meetings
  • Support my child's learning by fulfilling all responsibilities, including reading school communicatons like the Community Handbook and newsletter
  • Be punctual at arrival and dismissal
  • Attend at least one parent education event
  • Give 20 hours of volunteer service
  • Communicate with my child's teacher in a timely fashion
  • Abide by the guidelines of SAPMS Code of Conduct

Failure to comply with the Contract of Commitment may lead to my child's dismissal from SAPMS.

 

Check Volunteer Spot for service hour opportunities (see link below).  We have another Parent Education night and Community Meeting coming up in May.  New Parent Orientation will be offered in early June, and families who joined midyear can attend that. 

 
EARTH DAY

SAPMS students celebrated Earth Day by caring for our school grounds and street.  Children picked up trash on the campus and along Williams Street.  They also planted flowers and seeds.
