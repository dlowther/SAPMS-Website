Independence and Responsibility

Each Montessori classroom is a community of learners.  Children are expected to manage their own time, choose their own activities, and move freely about the classroom without disturbing others.  They are expected to interact in a respectful manner with both their peers and adults.  In order to do this, children must develop maturity and responsibility.  The successful learner in the Montessori classroom has the ability to self-regulate behavior, demonstrate self-motivation, and have an interest in learning.

In our classrooms, very little time is devoted to whole class instruction.  Instead, each teacher carefully designs a learning environment that contains activities uniquely suited to the children's interests and developmental abilities.  Students are free to choose their activities within the guidelines set by the teacher.  They are required to complete all work they start and return all materials to the proper place and in good condition.  As the children mature and develop, they become responsible for more tasks.  There is a dual emphasis on increasing levels of independence and responsibilities.

By giving your child increasing responsibility and independence at home, you can increase your child's intrinsic discipline and self-control.
  • Allow your children to manage their own take home folders, lunch boxes, and jackets.  Provide a defined space and consistent routine for this.
  • Give your child household chores, like helping set and clear the table, keeping his/her room neat, and any others that work for your family.
  • Help your child appreciate the value of their belongings by requiring that they are used for the intended purpose and cared for properly.

Healthy Lunch
I have noticed that many students are bringing a lot of processed packaged food for lunch.   We all know that good nutrition is a factor in learning and school success.  We ask that you do not send in sugary desserts and drinks.  Read labels and avoid foods with sugar in the first three ingredients.   We discourage the use of prepackaged "kids' meals" that contain an overabundance of sodium and preservatives and very little nutrition.  Gum, candy, soda, or sugary drinks like Yoo-hoo are not permitted.  We count on parents to work with us in teaching children about good health and nutrition.
