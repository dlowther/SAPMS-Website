International Day of Peace

Each year the International Day of Peace is observed around the world on September 21.  This day was established by the United Nations in 1981.  The UN General Assembly has declared this a day "devoted to strengthening the ideals of peace, both within and among all nations and peoples."  The theme for this year is Partnerships for Peace - Dignity for All.  The aim is to highlight the importance of all segments of society to work together to strive for peace.

The idea of strengthening the ideals of peace is a big part of Montessori education.  Cosmic Education, Montessori's hope for a peaceful future grew out of a wartime experience. At that time she wondered why there was no science of peace with a development comparable to the science of war.  Long before the UN existed, she saw education as a means for eliminating war.  Promoting values such as personal responsibility, respect for diversity, and global citizenship became part of the long term effort to establish lasting peace through the children.  A concrete pedagogy of peace was established that focused on the development of the whole child and valued creative and critical thinking.

Our school will gather on Monday, September 21 at 10:00.  We will hold a peace march down San Marco to the Mission.  Children will carry signs and banners.  On our return to school, we will gather in the main play area.  Children will share peace poems, and a song.  We will all share the Universal Greeting from the Gandhi Foundation.  This is a call and response with the following words:

I offer you Peace
I offer you Friendship
I offer you Love
I hear your Needs
I see your Beauty
I acknowledge your Feeling
My wisdom flows from a higher source
I salute that source in you
Let us work together
 
Please do not park on the neighborhood streets for this event.  School parking will fill quickly.  Please use the old CVS lot on San Marco or park at the Mission.  Before or after the march, we invite you to add you wish for peace to our Peace Tree.  We ask that everyone dress in white for this event.

 LE - Ms. Heather's Class
In Ms. Heather's LE class we have been busy learning about the scientific method with an experiment on the three states of matter. The children have been fascinated observing our Painted Lady caterpillars form into their chrysalis state as we begin our study of metamorphosis.
 LE - Ms. Gwen's Class
Art and community is part of the classroom environment in Ms. Gwen's class.  The students have talked about being a part of creating a  peaceful environment.   They  have had a lot of fun making peace signs and putting them together to create photographic art!  They have had some time now in class to get to know each other as a class community and what that means for them as a group.  It's rewarding to see them work together and create that peaceful environment so necessary for healthy learning.  

The class takes time to look around and observe their environment.  Each student has a sketch book and uses it to stop, study their surroundings, and sketch what they see.  It will be great to see their drawing collections at the end of the year.  This gives them an appreciation for observation and interpretation of everything around them.
 UE - Greetings from the Galimore Center Annex!

We've had quite a few busy weeks here in Lincolnville. We had our first successful field trip last week to the Tapestry Exhibit, which focused on the three essential cultural 'threads' that made up the early settlement of St. Augustine: the Timucua people, African Americans, and Spanish settlers. The children thoughtfully examined artifacts and documents, and got to explore a model Timucua thatched home, as well as a scaled replica of a Spanish sailing vessel. Thank you to the parent volunteers who helped the event go smoothly! Our classes have been diligently exploring new math endeavors, with the fourth graders beginning metric measurement, fifths working on decimals, and the sixth graders beginning algebra. Ms. Lorraine has led some intriguing field work in science, as the children have collected samples of aquatic life to observe and document, including tadpoles and dragonfly nymphs. We are working on preparations for next week's International Day of Peace Celebration by reciting peace poetry and creating collaborative art works. The children continue to be led by curiosity and academic hard work.
