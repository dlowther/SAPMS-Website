Parent Observations

As promised at the beginning of the school year, classroom observations will open starting next week.  To avoid having more than one extra adult in the classrooms, please schedule your observation with your child's teacher.  On the day of your observation, sign in at the office and wear your visitor tag.  Be sure to complete the little stub on the side with the date, time in, and time out. Sandi will give you a sheet with observation guidelines.  Remember to silence your cell phone before entering the classroom.  You will be given a place to sit, and we ask that you do not engage in conversation with the children. 

Remember that children's behavior often changes when their parent is in the classroom.  You may be surprised by the way your child responds to having you there.  Jot down any questions you may have, as the teacher will not be able to converse with you during or immediately following your observation.  Leave your question sheet in the office, and the teacher will get back to you.

Of course, you want to focus on your own child but also try to take in the entire classroom.  Listen to the noise level; it will usually rise and fall. It is normal to have a busy hum in elementary classrooms.  Notice the different ways children are learning.  What types of materials are being used cooperatively?  Where do you see a child working alone intensely? Are some children just observing? Notice the children's interactions and listen for the level of respect. Do you see children helping other children? 
Observe the teacher-child interactions and compare it with the traditional mode in which most of us were educated.  The Montessori teacher is considered a guide or facilitator.  Can you see why?  Our goal is have the child function as independently as possible in the classroom. Observe how a child chooses a piece of work, takes it from the shelf, completes it, then returns it to its place so it is available for the next child.  Notice the beautiful materials and how the cleanliness, quality, color, and placement attract the child to learn in the environment.

Children in the second plane of development, ages 6 - 12, are very socially oriented.  The Montessori classroom is set up to accommodate that.  Collaboration is encouraged.  Notice the little pockets of social activity that occur in the classroom.  This is a reflection of the child's natural desire to form relationships and be part of a community.

We encourage everyone to take time to observe during the morning work cycle, 8:30 - 11:30.  It's a great way to help you prepare for conferences coming up at the end of the month.  It will give you a much greater understanding of your child's work here at school.
